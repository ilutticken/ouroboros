## How "pleasant 8-bit" is made here (the research)

Classic NES sound is a fixed, tiny voice budget — and the charm is *arrangement within the
limits*, not more voices. This synth uses that same palette so the later 16/32-bit eras
**thicken** it rather than start over:

- **2 PULSE + 1 TRIANGLE + 1 NOISE.** Two square-wave channels (melody + arpeggio), one
  triangle (bass — softer, rounder than a square), one noise (percussion).
- **Duty cycle = timbre.** A pulse wave's on/off ratio is the tone knob: **12.5%** is thin and
  reedy (good for arps), **25%** is round and vocal (the melody), **50%** is hollow and soft
  (pads). Same oscillator, three characters.
- **Per-note ADSR envelopes.** The current in-game drone is unpleasant because it's a *held*
  oscillator with a tremolo — no note shape. Here every note has a quick attack, a decay to a
  sustain level, and a short release, so notes *articulate* instead of buzzing.
- **Arpeggios for chords.** The NES can't play a chord on one channel, so it sprays the chord
  tones as fast notes (root–third–fifth–third). Your ear fuses them into a chord — the signature
  8-bit shimmer.
- **A little vibrato** on the melody gives Cadenza's line a singer's waver (she's a diva).
- **Progression:** A-minor, `Am – F – C – G | F – C – G – Am` (i–VI–III–VII) — warm and wistful,
  resolving home. This is the through-line to build the whole soundtrack on.

## The layering plan (why this scales to 16/32-bit)

The design's audio ladder is *diegetic layers that switch on in-world*. These demos are built to
stack the same way:

- **Layer 0** — refreshd's bare tick (already in-game).
- **Layer 1** — Cadenza's Locked Groove: start with **02 (pad + bass)**, the quiet bed.
- **Layer 2** (Beat 8 reboot) — add the **arpeggio** and the **melody** → **01**.
- **Layer 3** (Beat 16, 32-bit) — add **percussion** (→ **03**), then countermelody, richer duty
  sweeps, and eventually real multi-channel detune/echo the 32-bit era can afford.

Cadenza "owns" this theme, so every later layer is *her* fanfare growing up — exactly the
narrative the design wants.

```
node audio-demos/chiptune.js
```

Edit `MELODY` / `ARP` / `BASS` / `BPM` near the bottom of `chiptune.js` and re-run. Notes are
`"A4"`, `"C#5"`, etc.; durations are in beats. Say what you'd change (faster? brighter? a
different key? a happier turn?) and I'll iterate on the WAVs before anything goes in the engine.
